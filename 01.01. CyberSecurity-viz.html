Core Concepts & Principles

Section Purpose

Here we explore the foundational pillars of cybersecurity: The CIA Triad, the Principle of Least Privilege, and the balance between Security and Performance. Interact with the Triad cards to see specific tools, and review the chart to understand the mathematical reality of Availability targets.

The CIA Triad

"Every security control on Earth ultimately props up one of these three legs. Lose a leg → the stool falls."

👁️ Confidentiality

Only authorized eyes see the data.

Tools: Encryption, ACLs, RBAC, MFA, Least Privilege.

Click to expand tools

⚖️ Integrity

Data read = data written.

Tools: Hashes (SHA-256, SHA-3), Digital Signatures, FIM.

Click to expand tools

⏱️ Availability

Reachable when needed.

Tools: Redundancy, Failover, DDoS protection, Backups.

Click to expand tools

🔑 Least Privilege

Default deny → then add the minimum. Codified in NIST SP 800-53 AC-6.

Key Takeaway: Least privilege is the cheapest, highest-impact control you will ever implement. Most breaches trace back to a violation of this.

⚖️ Performance vs Security

Every control has a cost: CPU, disk, latency. Captured in EZ01 element 1.4.

Key Takeaway: A perfectly secure system is unplugged and useless. A perfectly fast system is unprotected and doomed. The goal is balance.

Availability Targets (The "Nines")

"Available" is a number, not an adjective. Pick the tier the business needs and engineer to it. Notice the dramatic drop in allowed downtime as you add 9s.

Cryptography Primitives

Section Purpose

Cryptography is the mathematical engine of security. This section simplifies the three main primitives (Symmetric, Asymmetric, Hash) and their specific use cases, along with vital rules for handling passwords.

🔏

Symmetric

One Key

  • ✔️ AES-128/256
  • ✔️ ChaCha20
Fast → Bulk encryption
🗝️

Asymmetric

Key Pair

  • ✔️ RSA-2048+
  • ✔️ ECDSA, Ed25519
Slow → Key exchange, signatures
#️⃣

Hash

One-Way

  • ✔️ SHA-256, SHA-3, BLAKE2/3
  • ❌ Avoid: MD5, SHA-1
Fast → Integrity checks

"Asymmetric to bootstrap, symmetric for bulk, hash for integrity. That's TLS in one sentence."

🛑 Password Hashing Rule

A plain SHA-256 of a password is broken (no salt, too fast for modern GPUs).

Use a slow Key Derivation Function (KDF):

bcrypt scrypt Argon2id (OWASP Rec.)

Speed is the enemy when hashing passwords. Slow on purpose, salted always.
