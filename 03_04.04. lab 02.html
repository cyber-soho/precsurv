The Three Maps

What is physically connected to what?

  • Device names
  • Interface names (eth0, Gi0/1, vmnic2)
  • Cable types / virtual links
  • Switch port numbers

What speaks to what at Layer 3?

  • Subnets and their masks
  • Default gateway for each subnet
  • Inter-subnet routes (static or dynamic)
  • Any NAT boundary

What is allowed to talk to what at Layer 4+? (The Security Map)

  • Allowed protocols/ports between subnets/hosts
  • Authentication boundaries (SSO, MFA)
  • Data-classification labels (public/internal/confidential)

Most networks lack classification labels. Yours must have them.

Phase 2 Vulnerability Analysis

This section represents Part C. Armed with your three maps, you will systematically evaluate your architecture against standard defensibility principles. Findings from this checklist must be translated into actionable entries in your Architectural Risk Register.

Defensible Architecture Checklist

# Question to Answer Triggers Risk?
1 What type of network is this? / What topology is it actually? (Q1-2) If ambiguous
3 How many broadcast domains? If flat
4 Where is the user ↔ server boundary? If missing
6 Is administrative access on its own segment? If NO
8 Single point of failure — physical, logical, human? If found
10 Are any Zero Trust signals actually collected and acted on? If NO

The Risk Register Formula

Every "No" or "I don't know" in the checklist above becomes a row here. Require ≥ 10 rows for final submission.

ID: AR-001 | Finding: No admin segmentation | Where: 10.20.10.0 | Sev: High (7.5) | Remediation: Create VLAN 99 | Effort: M | Owner: NetEng
