Network Types & Topologies

Understanding the fundamental shape and scope of a network is the first step in defending it. This section categorizes networks by their physical reach and their structural layout, explicitly detailing the threat surface and failure modes associated with each configuration. The shape decides who eats first.

Key Takeaway: VLAN is not a network type. It is a segmentation construct inside a LAN.

Defense in Depth vs. Zero Trust

Modern defensible architecture relies on stacking controls and removing implicit trust. Here, we juxtapose the traditional 7 layers of Defense in Depth with the modern NIST SP 800-207 Zero Trust paradigm. Interact with the layers below to understand where specific controls live, and how Zero Trust re-arranges the internal perimeter.

🏰 Defense in Depth

Every control fails. Stack them so the next one catches what the previous one missed.

🔒 Zero Trust Architecture

"Never trust, always verify." (NIST SP 800-207)

1

PDP (Policy Decision Point)

The brain evaluating trust constantly.

2

PEP (Policy Enforcement Point)

The gate allowing or dropping traffic based on PDP.

3

Signals

Identity · device posture · context.

What it practically means

  • ✖ Inside-LAN ≠ credential
  • ✖ Login this morning ≠ ongoing auth
  • ✖ Managed device ≠ green light without posture
